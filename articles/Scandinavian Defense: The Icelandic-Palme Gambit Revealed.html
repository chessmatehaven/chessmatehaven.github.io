Understanding the Icelandic-Palme Gambit

The Icelandic-Palme Gambit arises after the moves:

1.e4 d5

2.exd5 Nf6

3.c4 e6

This sequence of moves deviates from the traditional Scandinavian Defense, where Black typically captures the pawn on e4 with the queen, leading to a solid and slightly passive position. However, in the Icelandic-Palme Gambit, Black boldly sacrifices the d5 pawn with 2...Nf6 and 3...e6, aiming to disrupt White's central control and create imbalances on the board.

The strategic concept behind this gambit is to undermine White's pawn structure and open up lines for Black's pieces, enabling active piece development and tactical opportunities. By sacrificing material, Black gains a positional advantage, putting pressure on White and setting the stage for a dynamic battle.
